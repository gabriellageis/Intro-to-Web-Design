STEM Opportunities for Young Women in High School

Girls Who Code is a nonprofit organization that is trying to increase the number of women in computer science. They are trying to achieve this by offering programs that give young women the chance to learn about computer science and gain skills and confidence in the subject. They are also trying to build sisterhood in the field of computer science. Girls Who Code offers programs such as clubs, college loops, and a free summer immersion program for young women. You can find more information about these programs on the Girls Who Code website.

Kode With Klossy is a free coding camp for young women that is offerred during the summer around the US. The program is designed to provide experiences and a learning environment that promotes interest in STEAM careers. STEAM is an expanded version of STEM that incorporates the arts, technology, and creativity into traditional STEM fields. Kode With Klossy teaches introductory computer science skills and promotes collaboration and creativity in the field. You can find more information about this program on the Kode With Klossy website.

iD Tech Camps is a summer computer science camp that teaches computer technology to both young women and men of ages 7 to 19. These camps are held in many locations in the US as well as in international locations. iD Tech runs a girls-only camp called Alexa Cafe that was created to decrease the gender gap in both the tech industry and in the iD Tech program itself. You can find more information about iD Tech Camps and the Alexa Cafe program on the iD Tech website.
